Background[]

The Cleanrot Knights were fierce warriors who remained devoted to Malenia, Blade of Miquella, even as her scarlet rot consumed their bodies.[1] Their winged helms and sword insignias embodied the unyielding resolve of their Empyrean master.[2] They stood in arms with Miquella's followers within Elphael, Brace of the Haligtree.

They fought under Malenia's command throughout her undefeated campaign in the Shattering,[1] which culminated in the fateful clash against General Radahn's Redmane army in Caelid. A majority of the knights fell at the Battle of Aeonia, their spirits locked in eternal combat in the War-Dead Catacombs. The few survivors wandered the resulting Swamp of Aeonia, while others traced the footsteps of Cleanrot Knight Finlay, who bore the sleeping Malenia all the way back to the Haligtree,[3] passing through the Shaded Castle along the way.

Chacteristics[]

All Cleanrot Knights wear golden armor and wield a Cleanrot Knight's Sword as a secondary weapon to complement their polearms.[4] This allows them to land multiple attacks in quick succession, triggering the Talisman's blessing to strengthen them even further. Most Cleanrot Knights wield a Cleanrot Spear, which bears a small shield on its shaft. They will imbue their spear with gold before performing their more deadly attacks, such as grabbing their enemy with the spear tip and infecting them with Scarlet Rot, and summoning forth a golden palisade from the ground.[5] They can also brace their spear with their sword to absorb an incoming blow and quickly retaliate with their own. Cleanrot knight commanders wield the Halo Scythe.[6] They prefer ranged combat, repeatedly firing rings of Miquella's light from a distance.[7] In close quarters, they may vomit Scarlet Rot.

Resistances[]

Cleanrot Knights are resistant to Slash and Holy damage, taking 35% and 40% less damage from these types. They receive flat damage from Pierce and Lightning damage, but take 20% extra damage from Fire attacks; however, the scythe-wielding variant can nullify this vulnerability by casting an incantation.

Background[]

A lone Cleanrot Knight, encountered in an alcove at the very end of Stillwater Cave that has been partially flooded in poisonous waters.

Strategy[]

Most of the room is full of poisonous water, so it's advisable to quickly run to the small island of safety just left of the boss, in the middle left to far left section of the room.

Since this boss is the spear-wielding variant and this fight will most likely happen early in the game, it's critical to avoid its Scarlet Rot-inducing, charging grab attack (signified by its spear glowing a bright gold). Background[]

A Cleanrot Knight and its commander, both encountered in an alcove at the very end of Abandoned Cave that has been partially flooded by scarlet rot. The remains of numerous Abudctor Virgins lay scattered around the arena.

Strategy[]

As the fight starts, only the spear-wielder is present, with the scythe-wielding commander rising from the rot pool to the left within 12 seconds of starting the battle. As such, it is best to spend that opening either summoning a Spirit or rushing down the spear knight to try and at least severely wound it before the scythe knight can join the fray.

Of the two combatants, the spear knight is more aggressive and will try to restrict player movement with its Sacred Phalanx and inflict Scarlet Rot with its charge. In contrast, the scythe knight will harass from afar with Miquella's Ring of Light and buff both of them with a unique combination of health regeneration and extra Fire resistance to cover for one of their weaknesses.

If fighting alone, try to pull the spear knight to the right so that the hill blocks the light rings and buys extra time to kill it.

Notes[]

  • The emphasis placed on the Cleanrot Knight's gait and cone-shaped helmet in concept art draws similarities to the Kindred of Rot.
