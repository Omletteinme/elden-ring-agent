Astel, Naturalborn of the Void is an optional Boss in Elden Ring.

Background[]

Far beyond the Lands Between, within the Greater Will's lightless abyss, the many-colored debris of ancient stars coalesced into a malformed creature.[1][2][3] A falling star of ill omen, Astel arrived long ago.[4] It assailed the Eternal Cities with a barrage of meteors,[5][6][7] sealing them deep underground, away from the true night sky.[8] It destroyed the Nameless Eternal City and Noklateo, the Shrouded City.[9][10][11]

Characteristics[]

A procession of multi-colored orbs, Astel is an aberrant, cosmic entity that resembles a Great Dragonfly.[12] It has a humanoid skull with large mandibles and a large blue eye emerging from a crack in its forehead. It has four translucent insect wings and six skeletal arms that end in human-like hands, the forelimbs each possessing six fingers. Its long tail ends in a spiny scorpion stinger, wielded as a flail. It also casts gravity-based magic and can warp to other parts of the arena.

Location[]

Astel, Naturalborn of the Void, is encountered within a secluded, starlit cavern somewhere along the underground Ainsel River, beyond the Lake of Rot. This area is accessed by entering the Transportation Coffin overlooking a chasm at the Grand Cloister. The elevator Astel blocks is the only path to the hidden Moonlight Altar, a necessary step for Ranni the Witch's questline.

Notes[]

  • Defeating Astel, Naturalborn of the Void for the first time unlocks the "Astel, Naturalborn of the Void" achievement.
  • Defeating this boss is optional, though it is mandatory for the "Age of the Stars" ending.
  • Astel resembles a Great Dragonfly.
  • Astel, Naturalborn of the Void, is the boss of the Grand Cloister, an area populated by insect-like Kindred of Rot.

Trivia[]

  • This boss was named Astel, Misbegotten of the Void in version 1.00. At the time, the Misbegotten were referred to as "Aberrations."
  • Astel has an unused animation in which it fell from the sky, crashing to the earth like a meteor. This move is used by Starscourge Radahn.
  • The internal data Astel's boss arena includes spawn markers for an Ancestor Spirit and its spirit animals, suggesting the Grand Cloister boss was reassigned to Astel from a third Ancestor Spirit encounter.
    • It is also possible that one of the Ancestor Spirit boss rooms was duplicated and used as a template.
