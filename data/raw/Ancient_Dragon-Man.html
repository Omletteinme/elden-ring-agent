Ancient Dragon-Man is an Invader and optional Enemy boss in Shadow of the Erdtree.

Overview[]

The Ancient Dragon-Man is enountered twice in the Gravesite Plain—once as an Invader and once as a formal boss. His Invasion triggers on the road southwest of the Pillar Path Waypoint Site of Grace. He is fought up the hill past Igon, who audibly cowers on the ground. The boss version is encountered at the bottom of the Dragon's Pit. Defeating him opens the path to the Foot of the Jagged Peak, which connects to Charo's Hidden Grave and the Jagged Peak.

Formerly a drake warrior who hunted colossal dragons, the Ancient Dragon-Man partook in the original form of Dragon Communion by consuming a jagged Rock Heart.[1] He took on the stony form of a small, bipedal ancient dragon and became the arbiter of those worthy to devour Bayle the Dread.[2]

Moveset[]

  • Wields the Dragon-Hunter's Great Katana and Dragon Communion Seal.
  • Uses the Magma Breath and Dragonmaw Incantations.
  • Prefers two-handed jumping attacks and Dragonwound Slash.
  • Has transformed using a Rock Heart.

Notes[]

  • Ancient Dragon-Man has a single Flask of Crimson Tears.
  • He has 0 Poise and 0% Damage Negation for each Damage Type.
  • Draconic Tree Sentinels also became dragons to better fight dragons.[3]

Strategy[]

It is possible to defeat the Ancient Dragon-Man without having leveled up or upgraded one's Weapons, Flasks and even Scadutree Blessings with relative comfort through the use of spamming the crouching light attack from Whips. As its long reach, fast attack-speed, low stamina-consumption and early stamina-regeneration, combined with the Ancient Dragon-Man's non-existent poise, allows the player to consistently harass him from a relatively safe distance without needing to worry about him trying to excessively dodge and then rush the player down like conventional projectiles from spells or bows would.

The player can increase their un-upgraded weapon's damage-output through the use of Blood Loss, Frostbite and Poison procs that are then immediately followed by the use of the Skill, The Poison Flower Blooms Twice as a third source of blood loss-like burst-damage.

The most efficient set-up for this would be to have two different whips they can alternate between. Hoslow's Petal Whip and Thorned Whip are excellent choices as they can both have their Affinities changed through the use of Ashes of War, while at the same time preserving their innate blood loss buildup no matter what affinity is chosen. So one Poison whip with Ash of War: The Poison Flower Blooms Twice and a Cold whip with almost any ash of the user's choosing.

Trivia[]

  • His humanoid appearance is that of a bald, middle-aged man with a wide jaw-line, while also carrying many visual traits distinctive to the Draconian race, such as their stone-black skin and eyes with grey irises and blood-red sclera.
